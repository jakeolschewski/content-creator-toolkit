## Hook Formulas That Stop the Scroll

The difference between a post that gets 100 views and one that gets 100,000 views almost always comes down to the hook. The first 1-3 seconds of a video or the first line of a caption determines whether someone keeps watching or scrolls past.

After analyzing over 10,000 viral posts across every major platform, we identified the patterns that consistently stop the scroll. Here are 20 of the most powerful hook formulas:

### The 20 Most Powerful Hook Formulas

**1. The Controversial Truth**
> "Nobody wants to tell you this, but [uncomfortable truth about your niche]..."

This works because it triggers curiosity and positions you as someone who gives the real information that others are afraid to share. People cannot resist finding out what the uncomfortable truth is.

**2. The Unexpected Result**
> "I tried [common advice] for 30 days and here is what actually happened..."

This leverages the gap between expectation and reality. Everyone has seen the common advice, so they want to know if it actually works when someone commits to it.

**3. The Authority Callout**
> "As a [credential/experience], here is what [audience] gets wrong about [topic]..."

This establishes credibility immediately and then creates curiosity about what mistake the audience is making. People want to know if they are making the mistake too.

**4. The Specific Number**
> "I made $[specific number] in [timeframe] doing [activity]. Here is exactly how..."

Specific numbers are more believable than round numbers and they create a concrete picture that the viewer can aspire to. The word "exactly" promises a step-by-step breakdown.

**5. The Time-Saving Promise**
> "Stop spending [long time] on [task]. This takes [short time] and works better..."

Everyone wants to save time. When you can show someone how to get the same or better results in a fraction of the time, they will always watch.

**6. The Pattern Interrupt**
> "Wait. Before you [common action], you need to know this..."

This creates urgency and stops people who are about to do the thing you are warning them about. It positions your content as essential viewing before they make a mistake.

**7. The Secret Reveal**
> "The [industry/platform] does not want you to know this, but..."

Conspiracy framing triggers deep curiosity. Even if people are skeptical, they cannot resist finding out what the supposed secret is.

**8. The Comparison**
> "[Thing everyone does] vs. [thing that actually works]. The difference is insane..."

Direct comparisons are inherently interesting because they promise to show you a better way. The word "insane" amplifies the expected gap.

**9. The Story Hook**
> "Last week I [dramatic/interesting situation] and it taught me [lesson]..."

Stories are the most ancient and effective form of content. Starting in the middle of action grabs attention immediately.

**10. The Mistake Warning**
> "If you are [common action], you are losing [money/time/followers]. Here is why..."

Loss aversion is one of the most powerful psychological triggers. People are more motivated to avoid losing something than to gain something new.

**11. The Before/After**
> "6 months ago I [before state]. Today I [after state]. Here is what changed..."

Transformation stories are endlessly compelling because they give people hope that they can achieve the same change.

**12. The Listicle Tease**
> "[Number] [things] that [desirable outcome]. Number [X] changed everything..."

Lists promise organized, actionable content. Highlighting one specific number creates curiosity about which item is the game-changer.

**13. The Question Hook**
> "Why do [successful people] all [specific habit]? Because [reason]..."

Questions engage the brain differently than statements. They create an open loop that the viewer needs to close by watching.

**14. The Myth Buster**
> "Everyone thinks [common belief] but the data shows [opposite]..."

Challenging conventional wisdom makes people stop because they want to defend their belief or learn something new. Either way, they watch.

**15. The Tool Reveal**
> "This [free/cheap] tool replaced my entire [expensive process]..."

Tool reveals combine the promise of saving money with the curiosity of discovering something new. People always want to know what the tool is.

**16. The Relatable Frustration**
> "Am I the only one who [common frustration]? Here is how I fixed it..."

This hook works by making the viewer feel seen and then promising a solution to something that has been bothering them.

**17. The Humble Brag**
> "I accidentally [impressive result] by doing [simple thing]..."

The word "accidentally" makes the impressive result seem achievable. If it happened by accident, surely the viewer can do it on purpose.

**18. The Countdown**
> "[Number] reasons why [bold claim]. Reason 1 is the most important..."

Countdowns create natural momentum and each reason is a mini-hook that keeps people watching for the next one.

**19. The Direct Challenge**
> "I bet you have never tried this [technique] for [goal]. Most [audience] have not..."

Challenges trigger competitive instincts. People want to prove that they have tried it, or they are curious about what they have been missing.

**20. The Prediction**
> "In [timeframe], [bold prediction about industry]. Here is how to prepare..."

Predictions position you as a thought leader and create urgency. People want to be prepared for what is coming next.

## Carousel Templates That Get Saved & Shared

Carousels are the highest-engagement format on Instagram and LinkedIn. They get 3x more engagement than single images, they get saved and shared at dramatically higher rates, and the algorithm loves them because they increase time spent on post.

But creating carousels from scratch is time-consuming. You need to plan the flow, write compelling copy for each slide, design the visuals, and make sure the whole thing tells a coherent story.

That is why we created 50 carousel templates that you can customize for any niche. Each template includes the slide-by-slide copy, the visual layout, design notes, and the caption to go with it.

Here are 10 of the most effective carousel frameworks:

### The 10 Most Effective Carousel Frameworks

**1. The Myth vs. Reality Carousel**
- Slide 1: Bold myth statement with "MYTH" label
- Slides 2-6: Each myth paired with the reality
- Slide 7: Key takeaway
- Slide 8: Call to action with your offer

Why it works: People love having their assumptions challenged. Each myth/reality pair is a mini revelation that keeps them swiping.

**2. The Step-by-Step Tutorial**
- Slide 1: "How to [achieve result] in [number] steps"
- Slides 2-8: One step per slide with clear instructions
- Slide 9: Before/after or expected result
- Slide 10: Save this and follow for more

Why it works: Tutorials provide immediate actionable value. Viewers save them to reference later, which signals the algorithm to push the post further.

**3. The Mistake Carousel**
- Slide 1: "[Number] mistakes killing your [goal]"
- Slides 2-7: One mistake per slide with why it is wrong
- Slide 8: What to do instead (summary)
- Slide 9: CTA to your solution

Why it works: Loss aversion makes people desperate to check if they are making these mistakes. Each slide reveals a new potential problem they need to know about.

**4. The Comparison Carousel**
- Slide 1: "[Beginner approach] vs. [Expert approach]"
- Slides 2-6: Side-by-side comparisons
- Slide 7: The key difference
- Slide 8: How to make the shift

Why it works: People naturally want to identify with the expert side. The comparison format is easy to scan and inherently shareable.

**5. The Data Story**
- Slide 1: Shocking statistic or data point
- Slides 2-5: More data points that build the narrative
- Slide 6: What this means for the audience
- Slide 7: What to do about it
- Slide 8: Your resource/product as the solution

Why it works: Data adds credibility and specificity. Numbers stick in people's minds and make the content feel more authoritative.

**6. The Tool Stack**
- Slide 1: "My [number]-tool stack for [goal]"
- Slides 2-7: One tool per slide with what it does and why you use it
- Slide 8: The complete stack summary
- Slide 9: Save this list

Why it works: Tool recommendations are some of the most saved content on Instagram and LinkedIn. People always want to know what tools successful people use.

**7. The Timeline Carousel**
- Slide 1: "My [timeframe] journey to [result]"
- Slides 2-8: Chronological milestones
- Slide 9: Lessons learned
- Slide 10: Your advice for those starting

Why it works: Timeline carousels are authentic and relatable. They show the real process, which builds trust and connection.

**8. The Checklist Carousel**
- Slide 1: "The [topic] checklist you need"
- Slides 2-8: Checklist items with checkboxes
- Slide 9: Bonus tip
- Slide 10: Download the full checklist (CTA)

Why it works: Checklists are inherently save-worthy because they are reference material. People bookmark them and come back to them repeatedly.

**9. The Hot Take Carousel**
- Slide 1: Controversial opinion in large text
- Slides 2-4: Your reasoning
- Slides 5-7: Evidence/examples
- Slide 8: The nuanced conclusion
- Slide 9: What do you think? (engagement CTA)

Why it works: Controversial opinions drive comments. Comments drive engagement. Engagement drives reach. The carousel format forces people to engage with your full argument.

**10. The Resource Dump**
- Slide 1: "[Number] free resources for [topic]"
- Slides 2-9: One resource per slide with name, link, what it does
- Slide 10: Follow for more resources

Why it works: Free resource carousels get shared more than almost any other format because they provide massive value with zero cost to the viewer.

## Faceless Video Scripts

Not everyone wants to be on camera. And you do not have to be. Some of the most successful content creators on TikTok, YouTube, and Instagram have never shown their face. They use screen recordings, stock footage, AI-generated visuals, text overlays, and voiceovers to create content that gets millions of views.

The key to faceless content is having a great script. When you are not relying on your personality or facial expressions, the words have to do all the heavy lifting. That is why we created 30 faceless video scripts that are specifically designed to work without a face on camera.

Here are 10 script outlines from the collection:

### 10 Faceless Video Script Outlines

**1. The "Things You Did Not Know" Script**
- Hook: "You have used [common thing] your entire life and never knew this..."
- Body: 3-5 surprising facts about the common thing, escalating in wow factor
- Close: "Follow for more things you did not know about [category]"
- Visual style: Stock footage with text overlays, zooms on key moments

**2. The AI Tool Review Script**
- Hook: "This free AI tool does in 5 seconds what used to take 3 hours..."
- Body: Screen recording of the tool in action, step by step
- Close: "Link in bio. Follow for more AI tools that save you time"
- Visual style: Screen recording with cursor highlights and text callouts

**3. The Money Math Script**
- Hook: "If you saved $[amount] per day, here is what happens in a year..."
- Body: Calculator screen recording showing the math, compounding effect
- Close: "Start today. The best time to start was yesterday. The second best time is now"
- Visual style: Calculator app or spreadsheet screen recording

**4. The History/Origin Story Script**
- Hook: "The real reason [popular brand/product] exists will surprise you..."
- Body: Chronological story with old photos, documents, key turning points
- Close: "And that is how [brand] became a [billion]-dollar company from [humble beginning]"
- Visual style: Historical images, newspaper clippings, animated timeline

**5. The Ranking/Tier List Script**
- Hook: "I ranked every [product/tool/method] from worst to best..."
- Body: Start with the worst, build to the best. Brief explanation for each ranking
- Close: "Do you agree? Comment your ranking below"
- Visual style: Tier list graphic, product images, rating overlays

**6. The Day-in-the-Life Script (Aspirational)**
- Hook: "A day in the life of someone who makes $[X] from [activity]..."
- Body: Walk through the daily routine, focusing on the business/creative activities
- Close: "This could be your life in [timeframe]. Here is how to start [link]"
- Visual style: Stock footage of lifestyle, workspace shots, screen recordings of work

**7. The Versus Script**
- Hook: "[Option A] vs [Option B]: which one actually wins?"
- Body: Compare across 5-7 criteria with clear winners for each
- Close: "The verdict: [winner] for [use case], [other] for [other use case]"
- Visual style: Split screen, comparison graphics, scorecard overlays

**8. The Process Breakdown Script**
- Hook: "Here is exactly how [impressive thing] is made, step by step..."
- Body: Detailed walkthrough of each step in the process
- Close: "Now you know how it is done. Save this for later"
- Visual style: Process footage, diagrams, step-number overlays

**9. The Prediction Script**
- Hook: "By [year], [bold prediction]. And most people are not ready..."
- Body: Evidence supporting the prediction, what it means for regular people
- Close: "The people who prepare now will be the ones who win. Here is how to start"
- Visual style: Data charts, futuristic stock footage, text overlays with statistics

**10. The Common Mistakes Script**
- Hook: "Stop doing this immediately. It is costing you [money/time/results]..."
- Body: 3-5 mistakes, what to do instead for each one
- Close: "Fix these today and watch your [results] improve. Follow for more tips"
- Visual style: Red X/green check overlays, before/after comparisons
